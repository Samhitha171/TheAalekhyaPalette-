The Beauty of Painting Hands

Samhitha Chandrashekhar · July 24, 2025 · 4 min read

Acrylic portrait — the painting that made me fall in love with hands as a subject.

There's something endlessly intimate about hands. They hold, they hesitate, they speak when words run dry.

This particular portrait is all about the hands. Well, at least for me. I started painting her, trying to shine focus on her eyes, her hair, and her facial features, but by the time I had finished the painting, I realised her hand was the main attraction.

"A hand can speak, even when a face is still. It can tremble in fear, reach in earnest, clench in anger."

Since then, I have come to realise that the hands are one of the most expressive parts of the human body. A hand can speak, even when a face is still. It can tremble in fear, reach in earnest, clench in anger. It can mean "I'm here," "I miss you," or "I let go."

Hands are honest. They don't pretend. The way they stretch or fold, how they age, how they move — it all tells the truth. That's why I love painting them. Not because they're easy. They're difficult, actually. Sometimes frustratingly so. Trying to express every shadow, vein, wrinkle and curve is a journey in itself. Painting them teaches me patience. It demands observation, anatomy, rhythm — and a little bit of surrender.

All of these made me observe people in real life — how a girl would wave her hands around when she's telling something that excites her, or a person fidgeting with their fingers when they're nervous. Sometimes a hand resting on a cheek says more than a smile ever could.

What fascinates me most is how universal their language is. We all understand a caress. A clenched fist. Interlocked fingers. It's a language with no words, but full of meaning. They're like quiet poetry.

Because hands —
They don't just do.
They feel.
They remember.
They become.
